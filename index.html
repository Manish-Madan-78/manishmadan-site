About

I make portfolios governable and PMOs functional.

Twenty years. Three continents. Portfolios ranging from a handful of initiatives to $120M+ spanning 50+ programs. The pattern is always the same: organizations that move fast outgrow their governance structures, and then they wonder why everything feels harder than it should.

What makes this different

Frameworks don't fail. Adoption does.

The hard part — getting a framework adopted, navigating resistance, making it stick across a skeptical organization — that's where most engagements fall short.

I've built relationships across C-suites and engineering floors, across financial services and technology, across three continents. That cross-functional fluency is what gets buy-in when a framework alone wouldn't. It's also what makes engagements move faster and land better.

At SVB, I inherited an enterprise PMO where project compliance sat at 75% and the framework had been presented to the business twice — and rejected twice. Within eighteen months of rebuilding the stakeholder relationships first, compliance was at 95% and the framework had been adopted organization-wide. The structure hadn't fundamentally changed. The trust had.

The results speak to the structure. The relationships make the structure possible.
